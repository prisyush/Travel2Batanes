Luñis

Luñis is a traditional Ivatan pork dish known for its simple preparation and long shelf life. It is made by slowly cooking pork in rock salt until the meat releases its own fat. The pork is then preserved in its oil, which helps it last for days without refrigeration.

Taste & Texture

• Salty and savory
• Tender yet slightly crispy when reheated
• Rich and flavorful

Why It's Special

• A traditional preservation method used by the Ivatan people
• Perfect for Batanes’ stormy weather when fresh food is limited
• Made with only a few simple ingredients
• A reflection of Ivatan resourcefulness and culture

Where to Try in Batanes

• Local Ivatan restaurants
• Small eateries in Basco
• Traditional family-run carinderias

Travel Tip

Luñis tastes best when served hot and crispy with steamed rice and fresh vegetables. It pairs well with coconut vinegar or local dipping sauces for extra flavor!
