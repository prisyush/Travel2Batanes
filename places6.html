House of Dakay

House of Dakay is considered the oldest surviving traditional Ivatan stone house in Batanes. Built in 1887 by Jose Estrella, the house was named after its longtime caretaker, Lola Ida Dakay. It has withstood strong typhoons and earthquakes, showing the durability and strength of Ivatan architecture.
The structure is made of thick limestone and coral stone walls, with a cogon grass roof designed to resist harsh weather conditions. Today, it stands as an important cultural and historical landmark in the province.

Location

• Located in the municipality of Ivana
• Found on Batan Island
• Within the province of Batanes
• Common stop in South Batan tours

Scenic Features

• Traditional Ivatan stone house architecture
• Thick limestone and coral stone walls
• Cogon grass roofing built for strong typhoons
• Surrounded by simple rural scenery and greenery
The house reflects the Ivatan people’s resilience and ability to adapt to the province’s extreme weather.

Best Time to Visit

• Morning or afternoon during tour schedules
• Dry season months for easier travel
• Usually visited briefly as part of a cultural heritage stop
Visitors typically spend 20–30 minutes exploring and taking photos.

How to Get There

• Fly to Basco Airport from Manila
• Join a South Batan tour by tricycle or van
• Travel by land from Basco to Ivana (around 30–40 minutes)

Visitor Tips

• Respect the property since it is a preserved heritage site
• Avoid touching fragile parts of the structure
• Ask permission before taking close-up photos
• Follow local guidelines during your visit

Why Visit

House of Dakay is a must-see destination for those interested in history and culture. It represents the strength, resilience, and traditional lifestyle of the Ivatan people, making it an important part of any Batanes travel experience.
