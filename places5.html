Rakuh a Payaman

Marlboro Hills (Rakuh a Payaman) is one of the most breathtaking viewpoints in Batanes. Locally known as Rakuh a Payaman, which means “vast pastureland,” this wide stretch of rolling hills is covered in green grass and often used as grazing land for cows and horses.
The name “Marlboro Hills” was given by visitors because the landscape resembles the famous countryside seen in cigarette advertisements. Its dramatic cliffs, open skies, and ocean views make it one of the most photographed spots in the province.

Location

• Located in the municipality of Mahatao
• Found on Batan Island
• Within the province of Batanes
• Usually included in South Batan tours

Scenic Features

• Wide rolling hills overlooking the sea
• Views of the West Philippine Sea and rugged cliffs
• Open pastureland with grazing cows and horses
• Dramatic sunrise and sunset scenery
On clear days, visitors can see endless green hills blending into the deep blue ocean, creating a peaceful and cinematic landscape.

Best Time to Visit

• Early morning for sunrise views
• Late afternoon for golden sunset lighting
• Dry season months for clearer skies and safer trails
Visitors often spend 45 minutes to 1 hour enjoying the scenery and taking photos.

How to Get There

• Fly to Basco Airport from Manila
• Join a South Batan tour by tricycle or van
• Travel by bike or motorcycle from Basco town proper

Visitor Tips

• Wear comfortable shoes for walking on grassy slopes
• Be cautious near cliff edges
• Expect strong winds
• Avoid littering and respect grazing animals

Why Visit

Marlboro Hills (Rakuh a Payaman) is a must-visit destination in Batanes because of its wide-open landscapes and peaceful atmosphere. It offers visitors a chance to experience the natural beauty, fresh air, and untouched scenery that make Batanes unique.
