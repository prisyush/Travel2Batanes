Valugan Boulder Beach

Valugan Boulder Beach is a famous coastal attraction known for its shoreline covered with smooth volcanic boulders instead of sand. The rocks were formed from past eruptions of nearby Mount Iraya and were gradually polished by powerful ocean waves and wind over time.
Despite its rugged setting, the beach offers dramatic scenery and a calming atmosphere created by the sound of waves crashing against the stones. However, it is not suitable for swimming because of strong currents and rough terrainea is also used as grazing land, so cows and goats are often seen roaming across the grassy terrain.

Location

• Located in Barangay San Joaquin, Basco on Batan Island in Batanes
• About a short ride from Basco town proper and near Basco Airport
• Found along the eastern coast (the name “Valugan” means “east” in Ivatan)

Scenic Features

• Shoreline filled with large andesite rocks shaped by the Pacific Ocean
• Landscape framed by cliffs, rolling hills, and sea views
• Loud rhythmic waves and strong winds create a unique coastal atmosphere
• Popular spot for sunrise photography and sightseeing
The beach stretches several kilometers and showcases enormous smooth boulders believed to have originated from volcanic activity and natural erosion.

Best Time to Visit

• Early morning or late afternoon for the best lighting and weather
• Dry months (March–May) are ideal for clearer skies
Visitors typically spend about 30 minutes exploring the area

How to Get There

• Fly to Basco Airport from Manila
• Travel by tricycle, bicycle, or join a North Batan tour
• Located only a few kilometers from Basco town center

Visitor Tips

• Wear sturdy footwear — the boulders can be uneven
• Do not collect rocks (prohibited)
• Be cautious of strong waves and winds
• Bring a camera for scenic photos

Why Visit

Valugan Boulder Beach stands out as one of Batanes’ most unique natural landscapes because of its volcanic history and unusual rocky shoreline. Its combination of geology, coastal scenery, and peaceful ambiance makes it a must-see destination for travelers exploring the province.
