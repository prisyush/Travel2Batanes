Coconut Crab

The Coconut Crab, locally known as Tatus, is one of the most unique and prized delicacies in Batanes. It is the largest land-living crab in the world and is known for its ability to climb coconut trees. The crab is usually steamed or cooked in coconut milk to highlight its naturally rich flavor.

Taste & Texture

• Sweet and rich seafood flavor
• Tender and juicy meat
• Slightly creamy when cooked in coconut milk

Why It's Special

• A rare and unique species found in Batanes
• A premium delicacy often served on special occasions
• Known for its impressive size and strength
• An iconic part of Ivatan coastal cuisine

Where to Try in Batanes

• Selected seafood restauran
• Local eateries in Basco
• Coastal communities (seasonal availability)

Travel Tip

Coconut Crab is not always available due to conservation efforts, so it’s best to ask local restaurants in advance. Enjoy it freshly cooked and paired with steamed rice for a true island dining experience!
