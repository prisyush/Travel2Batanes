Tukon Church

Tukon Church (Mt. Carmel Chapel) is a small but beautifully designed chapel located on a hilltop in Basco. Also known as Mt. Carmel Chapel, it was built through the efforts of the Abad family as a place of prayer and reflection. The chapel’s architecture is inspired by traditional Ivatan stone houses, using local materials that blend naturally with the surrounding landscape.
Aside from being a place of worship, it is also a popular tourist stop because of its peaceful setting and scenic views of the sea, rolling hills, and nearby mountains.

Location

• Situated in Barangay Chanarian, Basco
• Located on a hilltop overlooking Batan Island
• Within the province of Batanes
• Accessible by tricycle, bike, or as part of a North Batan tour

Scenic Features

• Elevated location with panoramic views of the sea and hills
• Overlooks Mount Iraya on clear days
• Traditional Ivatan-inspired stone structure
• Quiet and peaceful atmosphere ideal for reflection and photography
The chapel’s simple yet elegant design makes it blend beautifully with Batanes’ natural scenery.

Best Time to Visit

• Morning for cool weather and clear mountain views
• Late afternoon for softer lighting and sunset scenery
• Dry season months for better visibility and road conditions
Visitors usually spend around 30 minutes exploring the area and taking photos.

How to Get There

• Fly to Basco Airport from Manila
• Travel by tricycle, bicycle, or guided North Batan tour
• Short ride from Basco town proper

Visitor Tips

• Maintain silence and respect since it is a place of worship
• Dress modestly when entering the chapel
• Be prepared for strong winds on the hill
• Bring a camera for scenic views

Why Visit

Tukon Church (Mt. Carmel Chapel) offers a meaningful combination of spirituality, culture, and nature. Its hilltop location and Ivatan-inspired design make it one of the most peaceful and picturesque stops in Batanes, perfect for quiet reflection and sightseeing.
