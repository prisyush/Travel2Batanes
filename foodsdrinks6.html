Vunong

Vunong (also spelled Vunung) is a traditional Ivatan cooking method where meat, fish, or root crops are wrapped in taro leaves and cooked in a clay pot. The ingredients are slowly simmered, allowing the natural flavors to blend together. This dish highlights the Ivatan way of cooking using simple, natural materials.

Taste & Texture

• Mild and naturally savory
• Soft and tender
• Light yet flavorful

Why It's Special

• Cooked using traditional clay pots
• Uses fresh, locally grown ingredients
• Reflects the simple and sustainable Ivatan lifestyle
• Often prepared for family meals and gatherings

Where to Try in Batanes

• Local Ivatan restaurants
• Small eateries in Basco
• Traditional family-run carinderias

Travel Tip

Vunong is best enjoyed fresh and warm with steamed rice. Ask locals about the day’s ingredients, as the dish may vary depending on available seafood or vegetables.
