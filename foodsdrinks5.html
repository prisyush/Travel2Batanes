Dibang

Dibang is a small seasonal fish found in the coastal waters of Batanes. It is commonly sun-dried or fermented to preserve its freshness. This traditional preparation allows Ivatans to enjoy the fish even during stormy months when fishing is difficult.

Taste & Texture

• Salty and savory
• Crispy when fried
• Small but packed with flavor

Why It's Special

• A seasonal catch unique to Batanes
• Preserved through traditional sun-drying methods
• A staple food during typhoon season
• Reflects the resourcefulness of the Ivatan people

Where to Try in Batanes

• Local public markets
• Small eateries in Basco
• Traditional family-run carinderias

Travel Tip

Dibang is best enjoyed fried and served with garlic rice and fresh tomatoes. It also pairs well with coconut vinegar for an authentic Ivatan breakfast experience!
