Vayang Rolling Hills

Vayang Rolling Hills is one of the most iconic scenic attractions in Batanes, known for its wide slopes covered in lush green grass and panoramic views of the sea and surrounding islands. The landscape offers a peaceful and relaxing atmosphere, where visitors can enjoy open skies, cool winds, and sweeping coastal scenery.
Standing on the hills, travelers can see the West Philippine Sea, Mount Iraya, and even nearby islands like Sabtang and Itbayat on clear days. The area is also used as grazing land, so cows and goats are often seen roaming across the grassy terrain.

Location

• Found near Barangay San Antonio in Basco on Batan Island, Batanes
• A short ride from Basco town proper and accessible by road or tricycle
• Located close to Chadpidan Boulder Beach and Basco Airport

Scenic Features

• Endless rolling hills overlooking the coastline
• Strong sea breezes that ripple through the grass
• Views of rocky beaches and ocean horizons
• Ideal spot for photography, sightseeing, and quiet walks
The hills rise and dip gently and face the western coast, providing a dramatic perspective of the landscape and sea.

Best Time to Visit

• Dry season (March–May or early June) for clearer weather
• Late afternoon (around sunset) for colorful skies and dramatic lighting
Visitors usually spend about 30 minutes to 1 hour exploring and enjoying the view.

How to Get There

• Fly to Basco Airport from Manila
• Join a Batan Island tricycle or day tour that includes a stop at the hills
• Accessible by bike, motorcycle, or tricycle from town

Visitor Tips

• Wear comfortable shoes — slopes can be uneven
• Expect strong winds
• Bring a camera for scenic photos
• Follow local guidelines since some areas are farmland or private land

Why Visit

Vayang Rolling Hills is considered one of the most recognizable landmarks in Batanes because of its unique combination of green hills and seascapes. It offers travelers a rare sense of space and tranquility — perfect for relaxation, reflection, and experiencing the natural beauty of the province.
