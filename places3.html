Basco Lighthouse

Basco Lighthouse is one of the most recognizable landmarks in northern Philippines, standing on Naidi Hills overlooking the sea and the town proper. Built as a functional lighthouse to guide fishermen and passing vessels, it has also become a major tourist viewpoint because of its scenic surroundings and cultural design inspired by Ivatan stone houses.
From the top, visitors can enjoy wide views of the coastline, rolling hills, and nearby settlements. The structure’s red-roofed tower contrasts beautifully with the green landscape and blue ocean, making it a favorite photography location.

Location

• Situated in Basco on Batan Island
• Found on Naidi Hills near the coast
• Within the province of Batanes
• Easily accessible from the town center by short ride or walk

Scenic Features

• Elevated viewing deck offering panoramic ocean and town views
• Sightlines toward Mount Iraya on clear days
• Green hill surroundings ideal for sightseeing
• Sunset and sunrise photography opportunities
The combination of natural landscape and cultural architecture gives visitors both a visual and historical experience.

Best Time to Visit

• Early morning for cooler weather and clearer visibility
• Late afternoon for sunset views and softer lighting
• Dry season months typically provide better sightseeing conditions
Visitors usually spend 30 minutes to 1 hour exploring and enjoying the viewpoint.

How to Get There

• Fly to Basco Airport from Manila
• Travel by tricycle, bicycle, or guided North Batan tour
• Short distance from town proper, making it easy to include in itineraries

Visitor Tips

• Wear comfortable footwear for walking uphill
• Expect strong winds at the hilltop
• Bring water and sun protection
• Respect local surroundings and facilities

Why Visit

Basco Lighthouse is a must-visit attraction because it combines scenic coastal views, cultural architecture, and accessibility. It serves as both a functional maritime guide and a symbol of Batanes tourism, giving travelers a memorable introduction to the province’s natural beauty and heritage.
