Uved

Uved (also called Uvud) is a traditional Ivatan dish made from grated banana pith (the inner core of the banana trunk). It is mixed with minced pork or fish, garlic, onions, and local spices, then shaped into small patties or balls and steamed or fried.

Taste & Texture

• Soft and slightly chewy
• Savory and mildly sweet
• Rich in natural flavors

Why It's Special

• Made from locally grown ingredients
• Shows the Ivatan practice of using every part of the plant
• A healthy and fiber-rich traditional food
• Served during special gatherings and family meals

Where to Try in Batanes

• Local Ivatan restaurants
• Small eateries in Basco
• Traditional family-run carinderias

Travel Tip

Pair Uved with steamed rice and fresh coconut juice for an authentic Ivatan dining experience!
